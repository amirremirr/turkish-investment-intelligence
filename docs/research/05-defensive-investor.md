# Research Note 5 — The defensive Turkish investor

**A synthesis note: what the flow, rotation, and regime layers say
together about Turkish fund investors in mid-2026.**

## The setting

The regime engine reads the environment as: inflation 30.6% yoy and
falling, CBRT funding at 40% → **real rates +9.4pp (restrictive)**, FX
drifting (+5.2%/3m), equities up (+9.7%/3m). Deposits pay ~49%
nominal — a historically rare, strongly positive real yield on cash.

## Three observations that only make sense together

**1. Money is defensive.** 30-day category flows: Money Market
+₺98bn, Participation +₺28bn — while Equity Turkey (−₺6bn), Foreign
Equity (−₺6bn) and Precious Metals (−₺9bn) all bleed. The risk-asset
share of flows is 0.25 (risk-off boundary).

**2. Equity AUM share is rising anyway.** Equity Turkey's share of
total fund AUM rose from 5.4% (Dec 2025) to ~9% (Jul 2026) — *with
negative flows*. Decomposition: the share gain is entirely **price
effect**. Rising allocations in aggregate statistics do not mean
investors turned bullish; here they explicitly did not.

**3. This is rational, not fearful.** The regime engine's historical
table shows that in restrictive-rate months, the best median category
returns came from Precious Metals (+4.0%/mo), Money Market (+3.3%) and
Foreign Equity (+2.8%) — with money market delivering its return at
near-zero drawdown. Parking cash at +9pp real is defensible
positioning, not panic (breadth confirms: only 25% of stocks above
their 50-day average despite index highs — a narrow rally).

## The behavioral kicker

Note 2 established that flows chase **63-day trailing returns**
(NW t = 4.3). Equity's rally is, as of writing, ~3 months old. If the
pattern holds, equity inflows should *follow* the rally with the usual
quarterly lag — and Note 1 says those late inflows have historically
predicted weaker subsequent returns, specifically in calm markets.
The platform's flow monitor is the instrument for watching exactly
this handoff happen.

## Caveats

Single regime sample (18 restrictive months, 1 neutral); nominal TRY
returns; regime "historical winners" are descriptive medians, not
risk-adjusted recommendations. The synthesis is a lens, not a forecast.
